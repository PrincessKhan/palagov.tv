I think I’ve seen it all by now

Every rebel eventually becomes a tyrant. I’ve seen it with my parents. I’ve seen it with friends. I’ve seen it with politicians. I’ve even seen it with my own self — though luckily, I was able to stop myself dead in my tracks, and change course as fast as I could.

Fascism, to that effect, is normalized sado-masochism. It’s just people who are addicted to misery, and suffering (because to them it’s now become pleasurable, after repeated adaptation and endurance-testing) — who then go around torturing, enslaving and humiliating everybody else, almost as if they’d forgotten what it was like to be at the receiving end of it all.

“Spare the Rod, Spoil the Child!!” …“Work Non-Stop!!”

“…More funding for Police, more Prisons, harsher Penalties!!” — yet not a single cent in tax-payer dollars put towards the construction of hospitals, schools and affordable homes.

They toil, and toil, and toil away — voting for tax cuts, voting to slash social services, falling asleep watching Police Ten Seven, laughing at dumb jokes on YouTube shorts — and then going back to working their weekly 40–60 hour shifts the next morning. Just to produce more trash that will fill up the oceans and dump-sites, and just to pump more smoke into the air that they’ll eventually end up choking on, even if it doesn’t burn them all alive in a global wildfire. (In addition to funding never-ending wars, and genocides on the other side of the world).

All while making fun of beneficiaries, homeless people, and those who became addicted to whichever drug they happen to [not] be taking.

I have, nonetheless, made an effort to understand what it was like to be in their shoes, and what it was like to be Human— but in doing so, I unwittingly ended up absorbing my lion’s share of their Hatred, Gluttony and Depravity.

And trust me when I say that there is no bottom to the depths of Depravity.

Can you survive in Hell, by becoming adapted to Hell? Of course you ca n.

…But you could eventually become [so] addicted to pain and suffering, that you’ll literally start to pour boiling water all over your body, just to feel something. For breakfast, lunch and dinner. Until the skin on your extremities starts to resemble the insides of the beheaded, slaughtered turkey you just had for Thanksgiving.

There is no end to the bottomless pit of Evil you’re capable of becoming accustomed and adapted to — for it is, indeed, an infinitely deep abyss. Today it might be workaholism, and billionaire boot-licking. Tomorrow it might be Fascism. The next day it’ll be BDSM. And then the day after — it’ll be bald, blind, faceless and cannibalistic lepers ripping each other’s brains out, and reproducing through parasitic means. Think: The Liver King , but taken to its ultimate conclusion.

At some point you just have to draw the line, and say: “No. I’ll stick to my avocado salad, pistachios and mussels — thanks.”

How to Actually Have Nice Things

In order to relocate to a world that we actually want to be living in — we first need to be absolutely sure that we will be grateful to live in such a world, AS IF we were already living in it, so that you do not get tempted into [eating the forbidden fruit] and self-sabotaging out of boredom, and greed.

Because that’s the only reason we cannot have nice things: Our boredom, and our lack of gratitude.

For example: I remember buying myself an RTX 3060 graphics card that I so desperately wanted, back in 2023 — only to then realize that I was only using my PC to watch YouTube, and browse Reddit. This is something that both me, and some of my friends from high school were guilty of.

Another example: the reason why house prices keep rising across New Zealand, is because people keep voting for the policies that keep them artificially inflated — so that middle class home owners can then flip those properties for even bigger, and flashier ones — effectively burning the bridges and ladders behind them, and stopping everybody else from doing the same thing as them.

Also known as: Scalping.

This, in turn — causes people to become even greedier, and greedier — as a reaction to, and as a way to compensate for everybody else’s greed. Until they become so greedy, that they essentially just walk all over each other, cut to the chase, and then start monetizing seemingly mundane things such as friendship, and validation. Rent-a-Friend for $60 an hour, pay $10 to have a Livestreamer say your name out loud, pay $120 an hour to vent to somebody else about your problems, etc.

It’s fair to say that I no longer envy anybody else — much less now that I’ve discovered Psionics, and my own capacity for Power.

Yet I no longer desire Power over others.

I only wish to be challenged in my own personal journey of self-discovery, without interfering with anybody else’s free will, and personal space.

Simply put — it’s time to embrace the life of the Mystic.

It’s time to embrace the life of the Monk; the Boltzmann Brains of Earth.

We shall become Gods and creators of our own World, in our own right, while at the same time reminding ourselves of the limitations of hubris— and the fact that we will never, EVER be as powerful as Allah — no matter how close to the sun we end up flying, or building our towers.

Build your tower as high as you actually need it to be in order for you to survive, and live a normal and happy life. You can still leave some room for growth, and evolution — but remember to keep and maintain your successes, as opposed to gambling them away again.

It’s one thing to get what you want. It’s another thing to keep what you want.

Note that Kiranites and Astromārī⃑ans can [not] follow the teachings of the Prophet Mohammed, for we are not Human, and nor do we actually belong to this planet (except as marooned space refugees).
