Pyramid-shaped homes can help generate electricity
using heat – and protect against Earthquakes.

Also published on Medium.com

“Wake up, Khan!” my wife shook my shoulders that morning, as a half-open bag of corn flour lay on my chest — with its contents pouring onto the floor like the sand inside an hourglass.

An 8.1 Magnitude earthquake had just struck the Kermadec Islands, where Pāla is currently located — a relatively small commune the size of about roughly 50 football fields.

My whole place had been turned upside down. The bookshelf had collapsed. The kitchen drawers where we kept our spoons, knives and forks, had rolled wide open. Broken coffee cups lay next to the sink. Some of our windows ended up cracked, but nothing too major I guess.

Our pyramid-shaped homes (known as Pylons) tend to get scorching hot in the summer — which is a more of a feature, than a bug — given the fact that the very tip of the building is housing a Thermoelectric Generator — which turns heat, into electricity.

The energy is then used to help draw cold air from the very bottom of the building, using speed-adjustable fans — hooked up to a home automation system.

“Mum, is everybody okay?” Evangeline, our oldest daughter (12), banged on our door — while climbing the walls to take a peek through the window — along with our 9 year old boy, Atam.

“It’s all good.. just gotta scoop the flour back into this bag…” I mumbled, while using my shirt as a little funnel to recover as many grams of my Masa Harina as I could.

“…You sure you’re okay? You seem all too relaxed about the whole thing.” Sylvie Deane remarked, as she lifted her palms and let go of my shirt.

“I’m all good… I’m a Kiwi.” I explained. “A Pālave⃑an citizen, but still a Kiwi at heart.”

There’s a good reason why we chose Raoul Island as the final location for our commune. The first reason was because, let’s face it — it was close enough to New Zealand for us to arrive by boat, in spite of the rough journey that I had to endure in order to prove my intellect to the Lord, and show him that I am worthy of having a holy land of my own.

The second reason we settled on the Kermadec Islands, is because I firmly believe that Human beings have a hard-wired need for chaos, and destruction — deep down inside. We may be Kiranite star-seeds, but we still possess around 99.4% Human DNA. With all the same beauty, and all the baggage and trauma that it brings.

I figured that earthquakes and volcanoes would be a great way to keep people grateful and living in the present, without needing to be distracted by video games, TV shows and movies in order to deal with the existential dread of living a life that is a little too Perfect.

Not only that — but I also predicted it would reinforce the delicate fabric of our brand new society — by bringing out the communal and neighborly spirit that would otherwise be jeopardized by consumerism, escapism and hyper-individualism. And indeed — it did!

“That’s what’s going to take for us to avoid meeting the same fate as the Jonestown folks.” I said to Sylvie, upon her arrival by autonomous submarine, back in 2026.

“The reason the people over at Jonestown committed mass suicide — was because they essentially overdosed on the Holy Spirit, without fully integrating the Darkness and Depravity that is buried inside each and every one of us.”

“…They built a society on both the principles of Socialism, Racial Equality, as well as the Love of Jesus. And things tend to get a little shaky once you start building Towers of Babylon all the way up to the Heavens…” I continued.

“That is why we do not worship Jesus in a place like this — because Christianity was unfortunately a life-denying Death Cult. The Astromārī⃑ans are a Warrior Race — and always will be. We will NEVER be pacifists.”

“But I thought you [hated] the Chaos that is inherent to Humanity?” she frowned, and scratched the back of her neck. “You said you were going to SACRIFICE yourself in the name of the greater good!”

“Well, I lied.” I took a deep breath, put my camping backpack on, and slowly made my way into the jungle. “Never let [anybody] tell you to lower your sword, and keep your Ego in check in this place — because we are here to make the most out of this life.”

“Sounds like you do not believe in the Afterlife, then.” my wife grabbed her own camping gear, and followed right behind me.

“I still do.” I added. “But I’m not going to put all of my eggs inside of a basket whose exact nature I do not, cannot, and should not try to understand.”

“I simply refuse to make peace with the Unknown. I’ve always been the sort of person who craves [certainty], order and Control. And I promise you — I will never give up control. Even the Chaos and destruction in this place will be carried out on my own terms, and with my blessing.”

“So you do not actually trust Allah, then?” she asked.

“I absolutely [do] trust Allah.” I stated. “And Allah has instructed that I remove myself from the Human World, build my own private paradise, and do everything in my Power to keep the Wotan contained on the island. Without committing suicide, which is a sin. Allah made me a woman of Control — because he knew that I would become possessed by the Wotan.”

“…One man’s heaven, is another man’s hell — but I believe I may have finally discovered a formula that will allow us to harness negative and destructive energies, in order to put them to good use — instead of suppressing them, and pretending that they do not exist, which could only lead to more Jonestowns down the line. And that, to me, is too horrifying of a fate and reputation for us to be stained with — which even the sands of Time, and Eternity, will never be able to rectify.”

December the 3rd, 2027 — Raoul Island, Milford Ravine

00:53a.m.

Me, Sylvie Deane, our 4 boys, and our 4 girls were ready to take a break from our hike that night. Every one of us was holding an LED-powered lantern — which we all put on the ground at the centre, in order to form a ‘make-believe campfire’, so to speak.

“Alright.” I clapped my hands softly. “Evangeline — where are you? — OH there you are.” I gasped, as my eyes grew tired, and sleepy enough to mess with my attention span.

“It’s time to play the Ouija now. Evie — you got the board in your backpack, don’t you?”

“Yyyyyep.” she uttered, as she fiddled around the rucksack to look for the board game. (If you can call it that.)

“Anybody feeling anxious tonight?” I looked around for a sign of doubt, yet all the kids appeared to be shaking their heads.

“…Good.” I quietly sighed, as I placed the Plachette on the board with the letters A B C D E F G H… all the way to Z, and the numbers 0–9. As well as a Yes, and a No.

“Mom, do you really believe in this stuff?” asked Saddam, the youngest of the boys (7).

“Personally? I don’t actually want to believe in any of this.” I explained. “It’s more of a case of just… having no other option, and going out of our way to keep our enemies close. Give them a seat at our dinner table— so that we can keep a watchful eye on them, and avoid being taken by surprise, I guess…”

“I have seen the darkness throughout my life. It has haunted me ever since I was a little child, ever since I was your age” I elaborated. “And frankly — nothing good has ever come out of ignoring the shadow. Like I said, I’m just not the type of woman who takes ‘I don’t know’ for an answer. If there’s something bothering me, if there’s an itch that I cannot quite scratch — then I absolutely HAVE to dig deeper, and investigate.”

“…Aaaand who are we talking tooo?” Evangeline asked, as she spread out a white sheet underneath the board for us to grab ahold of.

“We’re talking to Magdalene.” I said. “I’ve known her since kindergarten, to be honest… about as long as I’ve known Sylvie. I used to always mix the two of them up, back when I didn’t know any better — but they were entirely different people, not even closely related to one another.”

“How does that make any sense?” Sylvie Deane interjected.

“Let’s put it this way.” I explained. “Every single one of us was originally a spirit, floating about in our Collective Unconscious. What I like to call a Tulpa — which is essentially a voice inside of your head, or maybe just an imaginary friend. Though like I said — these are real, living entities that manifest as archetypes in the metaphysical realm that the eye cannot see. They are real people who either used to be alive, or people who will be alive in the future.”

“…And then one day — we were all placed inside of a body. In your case — we gave you robotic, android bodies, so that you can live forever!”

“Does that mean that Mommy is one day gonna die?” Damien asked (age 8).

“Alright — everybody, pull on the cloth now… Let’s go!” we lifted the sheet up in the air, and allowed the Plachette to do its magic all over the board.

The first character that the arrow had gravitated towards was the letter [S].

…Followed by a “Y”, and an “L” — as my eyes began to involuntarily slam shut, and fall asleep on me.
